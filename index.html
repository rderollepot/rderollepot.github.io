Romain Derollepot

Engineer

EMob-Lab, Université Gustave Eiffel

I am an engineer at Université Gustave Eiffel (EMob-Lab, Bron), working on massive data exploration and scientific computing infrastructure. Trained in electrical engineering at ENSE3 (2010), I have progressively shifted my expertise toward data science, algorithm design, and HPC systems. I previously contributed to neurophysiological research at LESCOT, developing data processing pipelines for fNIRS (functional near-infrared spectroscopy). My current work focuses on large-scale GPS data processing, coordinating the PROMENADE computing platform, and promoting open and reproducible science practices.

PROMENADE

Scientific HPC platform for the EMob-Lab laboratory: 5 servers, 136 cores, 2 TB RAM, 2× Nvidia A100 GPUs. Responsible for daily maintenance and architectural evolution toward multi-user access.
